# Collaborators

This project is intentionally open to collaboration.

## How someone becomes a collaborator

You do not need prior permission to contribute. You become a public collaborator when you repeatedly contribute high-signal work that gets accepted and maintained over time.

Signals that matter:
- merged PRs
- strong review participation
- trusted issue triage
- security, performance, data-quality, or infra contributions
- public documentation and governance improvements that actually help the project

## Public collaborator expectations

Collaborators are expected to:
- work in the open
- follow [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md)
- respect [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- keep claims evidence-based
- help protect product quality and repo clarity

## Roles in practice

### Contributor

Opened PRs or issues but not yet part of the recurring collaborator pool.

### Active collaborator

Recurring, accepted contributions with review trust and visible project impact.

### Reviewer

A collaborator regularly trusted to review changes at a high standard.

### Maintainer

Has merge/release authority and policy responsibility.

## Revenue eligibility

Revenue sharing is governed by [REVENUE.md](./REVENUE.md).

In general, public collaborator eligibility requires:
- accepted contributions in the measured period
- attribution that can be linked to merged work or accepted review work
- no conduct or policy violations

Issues alone do not automatically qualify for collaborator revenue. Merged work and recognized review effort do.
